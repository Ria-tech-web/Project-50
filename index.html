Camel

  1. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  2. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  3. Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.

Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of the communication amoung Chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Cicada

  1. Cicadas have one of the longest lifespans for an insect.
  2. There are more than 3,000 Cicada species.
  3. Cicadas can buzz louder than a lawn mower.

Panda

  • Pandas have different eyes compared to bears.
  • Pandas spend 10-16 hours per day eating.
  • Pandas have great camoflouge in their environments.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  2. Pigeons have excellent hearing.
  3. Pigeons understand space and time.

Teddy Bear

  • September 9th is National Teddy Bear Day.
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
